The final session. We wove a live build through the entire class — starting from a conversation with Claude, moving through design, into code, and deploying a live site — while teaching the principles behind each phase.

"Most people skip to building. The magic is in the first two D's."

03 Decision — Clarity First

"The most important skill isn't building. It's knowing what to build."

You can prototype an idea in an afternoon now. That means the bottleneck isn't execution anymore — it's clarity. Knowing what you actually want. Before you ask the AI anything, ask yourself.

Decisions are cheap

With AI, you can test ideas fast. The cost of a wrong decision is low. The cost of no decision is high. Decide, build, learn, adjust.

The felt sense check

Week 1 principle, still true in Week 4: before you ask the machine, check in with yourself. What do you actually want? What problem are you solving? For whom?

What NOT to build

Deciding what to leave out is as important as deciding what to include. Scope is a gift. Constraints are creative fuel.

04 Design — Make Thinking Visible

"Design is a conversation between you and the future."

Design isn't about making things pretty. It's about making decisions visible — to yourself, to the AI, to anyone who works on this next. A wireframe, a PRD, a napkin sketch, a bullet list — these are all design.

The AI needs a blueprint

If you skip design and go straight to "build me a website," you'll get something generic. If you give it a clear structure — pages, components, content hierarchy — you get something that's yours.

Layers of fidelity

You don't always need a Figma mockup. Match the fidelity to the stakes:

  • → A bullet list — is design
  • → A conversation — is design
  • → A PRD — is design
  • → A full mockup — is design

05 Development — Taste + Hands

"You are the taste. The AI is the hands."

This is where everything from Weeks 1-3 comes together. The AI builds. You direct, review, and refine. The skill isn't writing code — it's knowing what good looks like.

Reading code you didn't write

You don't need to understand every line. You need to understand the shape. Does this look like what I asked for? Does the file structure make sense? Are there files I didn't expect?

The review cycle

Build → Look → Adjust → Build

Not build → ship. Build → look → adjust → build. Your job is the look and adjust part. The AI is tireless but tasteless.

CLAUDE.md

This file teaches the AI about your project. It's like onboarding a new team member. The better your CLAUDE.md, the better every future session goes. Compound knowledge — each project teaches you patterns.

06 Deployment — Make It Real

"Shipping changes who you are."

Before this cohort, most of you wouldn't have called yourselves builders. Now you've built things. That's not a certificate — it's a lived experience. Nobody can take it back.

"Done" beats "perfect"

Perfectionism is procrastination in a costume. Ship the version that works. You can always improve it later — and you probably won't need to as much as you think.

Build in public

Share the URL. Put it in your bio. Tell someone what you made. The act of sharing completes the creative cycle — idea → artifact → audience.

The skill compounds

Every project gets easier. Every conversation with the AI gets richer. The cohort ends. The skill doesn't.

07 Demo Day

The culmination of four weeks. Students shared what they built — not just the artifact, but the story of building it. What they set out to create, what they learned, and what surprised them.

The prompt for demos
