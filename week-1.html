// thinking_with_self

Thinking with Self

→ Know what you want. Before you can articulate it, you have to feel it.

→ Know your limitations. If you know what you don't know, you can ask for help — from humans or AI.

→ Stay balanced. These tools are exciting. They can sweep you up. Practices to stay self-connected matter.

// interoception

Your felt sense matters

In the world of vibecoding, your intuition about direction is crucial.

Being in touch with your body isn't separate from building.
It's how you know if something is right — even before you can explain why.

// thinking_with_others

Thinking with Others

→ We think better together. Other perspectives reveal what we can't see alone.

→ Articulation is practice. When you explain to a human, you feel how it lands. That empathic connection teaches you to use words others understand.

→ Others know things you don't. When you hit an edge, lean on people who've been there.

// thinking_with_machines

Thinking with Machines

→ Articulation is the skill. Everything you've practiced — knowing yourself, explaining to others — this is where it pays off.

→ AI is a collaborator, not an oracle. It doesn't have all the answers. It's not always right.

→ Your felt sense still matters. Even if you're not a coder — does this seem right? Trust that instinct.

// limitations

Understanding the limits

AI can be confidently wrong.

AI doesn't know what it doesn't know.

AI can't feel whether something is right for you.

This is why we lean on human others.
When you hit an edge you don't understand, find someone who does.

// key_principle

Context is everything

Bring more of your idea into the space.
The more context AI has, the more it can help.

Start by bringing it all out there.
Then chisel it down to find the right shape.

// tips

Some prompts to try

"I'm building [your idea]. Can you make me a simple landing page that explains what it is and why it matters?"

"Here's my idea: [describe it]. Before we build anything, can you ask me questions to help me clarify what I actually want?"

"I want to explain [your idea] to someone who's never heard of it. Help me find the right words."
