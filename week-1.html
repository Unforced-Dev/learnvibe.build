// why_claude

Why Claude?

→ Anthropic thinks about relationship. They care about how we relate to these models — not just what they can do. That makes them more sane to work with.

→ Opus 4.5 is the best coder. Pretty widely agreed — it writes better code than any other model right now.

→ The tools form a learning path. Desktop → Cowork → Code is a natural progression from simple to powerful.

// thinking_with_self

Thinking with Self

→ Know what you want. Before you can articulate it, you have to feel it.

→ Know your limitations. If you know what you don't know, you can ask for help.

→ Stay balanced. These tools are exciting and can sweep you up. Staying self-connected matters.

Your felt sense of direction is crucial. Being in touch with your body isn't separate from building — it's how you know if something is right.

// thinking_with_others

Thinking with Others

→ We think better together. Other perspectives reveal what we can't see alone.

→ Articulation is practice. When you explain to a human, you feel how it lands. That teaches you to use words others understand.

→ This cohort is the container. I don't have all the answers. We lean on each other and build relationships in the process.

// thinking_with_machines

Thinking with Machines

→ Articulation is the skill. Everything you practice with self and others pays off here.

→ Context is everything. The more you bring in, the more AI can help. Start big, then chisel down.

→ Know the limits. AI can be confidently wrong. It doesn't know what it doesn't know. Your felt sense still matters — even if you're not a coder.

When you hit an edge you don't understand, that's when you lean on human others.

// hands_on

Your turn

Open Claude Desktop (or claude.ai). Tell it about your idea. Ask it to make you something.

"I'm building [your idea]. Can you make me a simple landing page that explains what it is?"

"Here's my idea: [describe it]. Before we build, can you ask me questions to help me clarify?"

"I want to explain [your idea] to someone new. Help me find the right words."
