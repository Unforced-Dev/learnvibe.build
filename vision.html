What Learn Vibe Build
is becoming.

This is a living document — a candid look at where LVB is heading, how it fits into a larger vision, and what we're building together. It's meant for the people close to this work: collaborators, partners, RegenHub members, and the builders who came through Cohort 1.

Last updated March 2026

// the_insight

Everyone is a builder

People coming through LVB are not necessarily learning to be engineers or app developers. They're learning to use emerging AI technologies to be more effective creators and builders in the world.

They may be a small business owner wanting to build their landing page or e-commerce platform. An artist wanting to create their website and social media strategy. Someone trying to eat healthier and build better practices. A writer with aspirations of structuring a book and giving it form.

Everyone is creative in some capacity and everyone has gifts they want to bring into the world. There is a possibility of using AI tooling to help them be more effective at that.

The idea is that everyone is creative in some capacity and everyone has gifts they want to bring into the world. LVB is about forming a collaborative relationship with technology — especially AI — to become a more effective agent for change in the world.

This isn't about prompt engineering tricks. It's about learning to articulate what you want, think clearly about what you're building, and use AI as a genuine creative partner to bring it into form.

// cohort_2

The next cohort

Early April 2026 · 6 weeks

We ran Cohort 1 in January, sponsored by Gitcoin — and it worked. People who had never written a line of code shipped real projects. The energy was strong, the community was real, and it was clear this format has legs.

Cohort 2 expands to 6 weeks, with more depth and more space for building. We're aiming for early April, with advertising starting this week.

Format

weekly

1–2 hour sessions — shorter with pre-recorded lectures, longer when done live. Core curriculum and demonstration.

weekly

2 hour co-work & office hours — open working time together, hands-on help, peer learning.

ongoing

Community platform — share projects, ask questions, get inspired by what others are building.

Facilitators

Aaron Gabriel and Jon as lead facilitators, with the possibility of Lucian as a third. Multiple facilitators means more perspectives and more support for participants.

Pricing

General admission $500
Cohort 1 alumni $250
RegenHub co-working members $250
RegenHub core members Free

This is an introductory price — the plan is for future cohorts to be $1,000. We're keeping it accessible as we build the community.

Revenue model

Revenue splits 60% to facilitators and 40% to the cooperative. With 10–15 people paying full price, we're looking at $5–10k from this cohort — a meaningful start that funds both the teaching and the infrastructure.

The goal isn't just revenue. It's building a strong community of practice. That's why we're bringing in core members and alumni at reduced rates — their experience and engagement strengthens the entire cohort.

// the_platform

Beyond cohorts

The cohort model is how we bridge people in. But the thing we're actually building is an ongoing community of practice — a place where people create and learn together, long after any single cohort ends.

The next major step is building out the platform. Our own version of what Circle, Teachable, and similar tools do — but built for this community, by this community:

Project sharing

A way for people to share what they're building and be inspired by each other's work.

Learning forum

Space to share discoveries, ask questions, and build collective knowledge.

Cohort curriculum

Structured course content that lives on and remains accessible to alumni.

Community hub

The connective tissue between cohorts — where the community actually lives.

Some of this is already built. Much of it remains to be done. The immediate steps: onboard Cohort 1 alumni onto the platform, and prepare for Cohort 2.

Inspirations we're drawing from: Tinkerer.club and Jonny Miller's Nervous System Mastery cohort model.

// the_bigger_picture

Part of something larger

LVB is a program of RegenHub, the cooperative workspace at 1515 Walnut in Boulder. RegenHub is gaining real momentum — the co-working space is growing, membership is expanding, and the community is strengthening.

This positions us as an AI-first cooperative: a cooperative looking at how to use technology well as a way to benefit people and planet.

The Techne Vision

Holding the vision. Growing into it.

We see the potential for all of this to eventually become Techne — from the Greek for craft, art, practical wisdom. A cooperative dedicated to building technology differently, innovating wisely, and helping to point a different direction for where technology can go.

→ Techne Cooperative — the organizational structure, member-owned and governed
→ Techne Foundation — the non-profit arm
→ Techne Institute — the learning center (where LVB lives)
→ Techne Studios — creative production and making
→ Techne Builders — technology development and services

The brands we have — RegenHub, Learn Vibe Build — already have recognition and momentum. We're not rebranding overnight. We're growing into this vision organically, starting in Boulder and extending beyond.

Taking the LVB cohort is also a step toward cooperative membership — which means having a say in how funds are allocated and how we grow together. Right now it doesn't make sense to require the cohort for co-working members, but we're planting the seeds. That's why existing co-workers get the cohort at a discount — we're building the culture from where we are.
